Fermented Hot Sauce

Ingredients:

  • ~15 small sweet peppers
  • 3 habaneros
  • 3 Fresno chilis
  • 2 ripe red jalapeños
  • 4 smashed garlic cloves
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Olive oil
  • Raw sugar

Instructions:

  1. Prepare jar: Start with a clean big jar that has a solid fitting lid.
  2. Choose and prep peppers: Assemble a mixture of small sweet peppers and hot peppers—red jalapeños, Fresnos, and habaneros are great. Keep final hot sauce color in mind.
  3. Remove seeds and veins: On a cutting board, remove seeds and veins from sweet peppers and most of the seeds and veins from hot peppers. Adjust spice level to taste.
  4. Weigh peppers and water: Place empty jar on a baking scale, zero it, then add prepped peppers and garlic. Cover with filtered water and record the total weight.
  5. Calculate salt: Multiply the total weight by 0.02 to get 2% of the total weight. This is your salt amount.
  6. Dissolve salt: Weigh out salt and dissolve in some filtered water warmed in a pan. Add back to the jar and mix gently.
  7. Ferment: Seal the jar and place in a dark, cool location. Let ferment for 1–2 weeks.
  8. Blend into sauce: Once fermented, transfer everything to a blender. Add 1/3 cup olive oil and 1.5 tablespoons raw sugar. Blend until smooth. Adjust seasoning with more sugar, salt, or pepper to taste.
