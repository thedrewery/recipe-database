Drew’s Gluten Free, Dairy Free Ramen Smash Burgers

Ingredients:

  • 1–2 large garlic cloves
  • 1 frozen ginger cube
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes
  • 1 tablespoon toasted sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons peanut oil
  • 2 bricks brown rice ramen noodles
  • Salt, to season water and taste
  • 1 beaten egg
  • 1 teaspoon salt (for seasoning noodles)
  • 1/3 medium yellow onion, very thinly sliced (mandolin recommended)
  • Neutral spray oil (avocado oil recommended)
  • Drizzle of soy sauce (gluten-free, if needed)
  • 1 lb ground beef, divided into four 1/4 lb patties
  • 1 scallion, chopped
  • Black pepper, to taste
  • Cheddar cheese slices (dairy-free if needed)
  • Sriracha mayo (dairy-free if needed)

Instructions:

  1. Make aromatic oil: Sauté 1–2 large garlic cloves, 1 frozen ginger cube, and a pinch of red pepper flakes in 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil and 2 tbsp peanut oil until bubbling and frothy—but not browned. Set aside to cool.
  2. Cook noodles: Boil 2 bricks of brown rice ramen noodles in salted water for 2 minutes. Use a fork to gently separate the noodles while boiling. Drain into a bowl using a colander and continue separating as they cool. Noodles should still be slightly stiff. Pour hot seasoned water (from straining) over the noodles to finish softening, then set aside.
  3. Combine noodles and binder: Once oil is cool, mix with the cooked and separated noodles, 1 teaspoon salt, and 1 beaten egg. Stir until noodles are evenly coated.
  4. Form ramen patties: Divide noodles evenly into 4 small molds (like pot pie tins) and chill uncovered in the fridge for at least 2 hours or overnight to set.
  5. Prep toppings: Use a mandolin to shave 1/3 of a medium yellow onion into very thin slices. Chop 1 scallion for burger topping.
  6. Cook ramen buns: On a medium heat griddle, cook ramen patties with a drizzle of soy sauce and a spray of avocado oil until both sides are crispy and they hold their shape.
  7. Smash burgers: Increase griddle to high heat. Once hot and nearly smoking, place ground beef patties down and top with shaved onions and chopped scallion. Smash firmly into the griddle. Season both sides with salt and pepper. When nearly done, top with cheddar cheese.
  8. Assemble and serve: Serve burgers between crispy ramen buns with sriracha mayo. Enjoy!
