Nonna Tomato Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup good olive oil
  • 5–7 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • A handful of fresh basil leaves
  • 1 large onion, minced
  • 2–4 shallots, minced
  • Salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Cherry tomatoes (fresh or leftover), halved
  • 12 oz red wine (e.g., Pinot Noir)
  • 1 (28 oz) can San Marzano tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
  • 1 tablespoon raw sugar
  • Optional: additional salt, pepper, acid, or sweetener to adjust flavor

Instructions:

  1. Infuse the oil: Heat 1/4 cup olive oil in a large saucepan with 5–7 thinly sliced garlic cloves and a generous handful of basil leaves. Cook on low until garlic is just browning and basil looks crispy.
  2. Remove aromatics: Use a slotted spoon to remove the garlic and basil and set them aside in a small bowl.
  3. Sauté onions and shallots: Using the infused oil, sauté 1 large minced onion and 2–4 minced shallots. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper.
  4. Add tomatoes: Once the onions are translucent, add halved cherry tomatoes and sauté for a few minutes.
  5. Deglaze with wine: Add at least 12 oz of red wine and simmer to let the alcohol cook off.
  6. Add canned tomatoes and oregano: Stir in one 28 oz can of San Marzano tomatoes and 1 teaspoon dried oregano.
  7. Simmer: Let the sauce simmer for about an hour, stirring occasionally.
  8. Return garlic and basil: Add the reserved garlic and basil along with any leftover infused oil.
  9. Balance flavors: Add the juice of 1/2 lemon and 1 tablespoon raw sugar. Simmer for at least another hour, adjusting salt, pepper, sweetness, or acidity to taste.
