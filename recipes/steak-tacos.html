Nick Shipp’s Steak Tacos

(Can’t Call It Carne Asada)

Nick Shipp is a professional chef and a friend — we met years ago at Burgers and Brew Fest when I was the head brewer for Santa Monica Brew Works, and he was the chef at Upper West, which had our beer on tap. He was nice enough to give me this recipe via text when I contacted him years later, and in addition to how good it tastes, the story behind it makes it even better. “Yeah those steak tacos have been on that menu forever. I actually do them at the club here at Aliso too. The meat is a skirt steak and the marinade is a blend of balsamic vinegar, soy sauce, brown sugar, Maggi seasoning, a lot of cilantro, smoked paprika, ground cumin, olive oil, and water. 48 hours is good.”

Ingredients:

  • 3 lbs skirt steak
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup turbinado sugar dissolved in 2 Tbsp hot filtered water
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1.5 tsp cumin
  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 2.5 Tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • Most of 1 bunch cilantro, chopped (include some stems)
  • 1 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • A few dashes Maggi seasoning sauce
  • 1.5 tsp salt
  • 3 minced garlic cloves
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1/2 medium red onion, grated

Instructions:

  1. Combine: Combine all marinade ingredients in a large bowl or zip-top bag. Make sure the steak is fully coated.
  2. Marinate: Refrigerate for 48 hours if possible.
  3. Grill: Grill on medium-high heat for about 4 minutes per side, or until nicely charred and cooked to your preferred doneness.
  4. Serve: Slice against the grain and serve as tacos with warm corn tortillas and salsa or chimichurri.
