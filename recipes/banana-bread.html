Gluten-Free Banana Bread

Ingredients:

  • 2 overripe bananas
  • 50 g turbinado sugar
  • 3 Tbsp maple syrup
  • 1/2 Tbsp vanilla extract
  • 90 g olive oil
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 tsp Celtic sea salt
  • 180 g oat flour
  • 50 g Bob’s Red Mill 1:1 gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • Butter, for greasing the pan
  • Pine nuts, walnut pieces, pumpkin seeds, or a combination (for topping)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven: Heat oven to 345°F. Butter an 8x5-inch loaf pan and line it with parchment paper.
  2. Mix the wet ingredients: In a medium bowl, mash the overripe bananas, then whisk in turbinado sugar, maple syrup, vanilla extract, olive oil, and eggs until smooth and well combined.
  3. Mix the dry ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together sea salt, oat flour, gluten-free all-purpose flour, baking soda, and cinnamon.
  4. Combine: Add the dry mixture to the wet ingredients and stir just until everything is evenly incorporated—do not overmix.
  5. Fill the pan: Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top. Sprinkle with pine nuts, walnut pieces, pumpkin seeds, or a mix of all three.
  6. Bake: Bake at 345°F for 40–50 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean or with just a few moist crumbs.
  7. Cool and serve: Let the banana bread cool in the pan for a bit, then lift out using the parchment and transfer to a rack to cool completely before slicing.
